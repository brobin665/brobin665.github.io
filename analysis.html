GIS Analysis Projects

Wisconsin Milk Cow Density Study

Wisconsin Milk Cow Density Study, Geography 390, UW Stevens Point

This artifact is the result of a series of exercises based on the density of the milk cow population in Wisconsin over a set of years. This map series with the blue color scale depicts density of milk cows using the Equal Interval classification by square miles for each year of the data set. This series of exercises taught me about the differences associated with each type of classification, and when it is best to use a classification type.

Forest Cover Fragmentation

Forest Cover Fragmentation of the Township of Stark, Geography 479, UW Stevens Point

The purpose of this project was to use the Patch Grid extension in ArcMap to develop a series of spatial statistics that summarizes the landscape morphology of the Township of Stark from a series of years between 1939 and 1995. The statistics were then applied to a template to create the artifact listed. This lab taught me about the use of extensions in ArcMap for specific purposes, and about patch analysis of a landscape.

Sandhill Wildlife Management

Sandhill Wildlife Management Area, Geography 479,UW Stevens Point

This was a class project which looked at the change in habitat preference of the ruffed grouse population at the Sandhill Wildlife Management Area in Wood County, WI, over a seven year period between 1992 and 1998. The analysis performed is called a weighted cartographic model, which will determine the ideal habitat of the ruffed grouse during the seven year period. ArcMap was the program used to run the analysis and compile the map document. I chose this artifact as it combines analysis with the cartographic challenge of displaying multiple maps in one layout. It was also my first time using a weighted cartographic model. Since completing the class, I corrected errors I made in the labeling and the layout of the final map.
